Hennen van Merchtenen and De Voortzetting

Three lines of computational evidence toward a shared author between Hennen van Merchtenen's work and De Voortzetting van de Brabantsche Yeesten.

Bram Bakker ·

Abstract

De Voortzetting van de Brabantsche Yeesten is a fifteenth-century Middle Dutch rhyme chronicle detailing the history of the Duchy of Brabant. Written as a direct sequel to the earlier Brabantsche Yeesten by Jan van Boendale, its authorship remains a subject of active scholarly debate. A prominent candidate proposed by scholars is Hennen van Merchtenen, who authored a similar chronicle on the subject in 1415, titled the Cornicke van Brabant.

De Voortzetting survives in a remarkable autograph manuscript filled with marginal additions, deletions, and corrections, providing rare insight into the author’s writing and editing process Houthuys (2004). Unfortunately no such autograph exists for Merchtenen's Cornicke. Consequently, handwriting comparison cannot be used to settle the identity of the author.

Building on the work of Reynaert (2019), who argued for Merchtenen’s authorship based on (among other evidence) qualitative stylistic and rhyming evidence, this project approaches the problem computationally. Using word frequencies and machine learning representations, we assess and compare the writing styles of these chronicles against a wider corpus of Middle Dutch rhymed texts.

How it was made

Three independent methods were run over a corpus of 263 Middle Dutch rhymed manuscripts: TF‑IDF bigram/trigram cosine similarity, rhyme-word cosine similarity, and a (not working) contrastive character-level line encoder (based on ByT5) trained across the corpus with author identity as the training signal. For the first two methods, each questioned book's similarity to the known text is checked against a background distribution — all document similarity to every other document in the corpus — so a high score can be judged against how much similarity naturally varies between unrelated documents. The full pipeline and how to regenerate the data behind these tabs is documented in the README.
